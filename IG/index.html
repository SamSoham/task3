As per the latest MCDR guidelines, all plans and sections related to mine shall be prepared by combination of Digital Global Positioning System (DGPS) or Total Station or by Drone survey in relation to certain or all leases as may be specified by Indian Bureau of Mines (IBM, Nagpur).

New Rule inserted to provide for submission of digital images of mining areas by lessees and Letter of Intent holders. Lessees having annual excavation plans of 1 million tonne or more or having leased area of 50 hectare or more are required to submit drone survey images of leased area and up to 100 meters outside the lease boundary every year.

Financial Reduction of Compliance Burden

Provision of daily return omitted to reduce compliance burden. Power of taking action against incomplete or wrong or false information in monthly or annual returns given to IBM, in addition to State Government.

Compliance with MCDR Rules SOP

Complying with MCDR Standard Operating Procedures under sub rule(5) of Rule 34A for carrying out drone survey and submission of Digital Aerial Images of Mining Areas to Indian Bureau of Mines is essential for safe and legal drone operations in Mining Areas. At IG Drones we follow strict compliance processes to ensure that our operations meet all the requirements under the MCDR Rules. Here are some steps we take to ensure compliance:

Obtaining necessary approvals and licenses: Before conducting any drone operations, we obtain all the necessary approvals and licenses from the relevant authorities including DGCA.

Maintaining records:We maintain detailed records of all our operations, including flight logs, maintenance records, and more. This helps us stay compliant with the MCDR Rules and provides transparency to our clients
